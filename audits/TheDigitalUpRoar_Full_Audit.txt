5. Pricing Strategy — Should They Show Prices?
This is the most common strategic question for boutique service businesses. Here is the complete analysis
for The Digital UpRoar's specific situation.

The Case FOR Showing Pricing
• Pre-qualifies leads automatically — eliminates discovery calls with price-sensitive buyers who
aren't a fit
• Reduces friction — many buyers need a number before they'll start a conversation
• Signals transparency and confidence — builds trust vs. competitors who hide pricing
• SEO asset — 'virtual tour pricing' and 'virtual tour cost' are high-intent buyer search queries
• Works well for productized tiers (Essential / Professional / Signature)

The Case AGAINST Showing Pricing

• Projects vary significantly — a flat price can undervalue complex multi-location jobs or scare off
simple ones
• Competitors can undercut once rates are visible
• Enterprise hospitality buyers (TAO Group-tier) expect custom pricing — a $500 tier anchor could
work against you
• May reduce premium positioning perception if your actual prices are lower than what a prospect
assumed

The Recommendation: Tiered Anchor Pricing with 'Starting From'
The verdict: Show pricing — but use starting-from anchors, not fixed prices.

This captures both benefits: it pre-qualifies budget-sensitive buyers AND keeps high-end custom
deals fully open. Here is the recommended tier structure:

Essential Tour — "Starting from $X" (basic space capture, Google Maps integration, 1 location)
Professional Tour — "Starting from $X" (custom branding, hotspots, narration, social delivery
assets)
Signature Tour — "Custom Quote" (full production, VR compatibility, multi-location, white-glove
service)

Key rule: "Starting from" sets a floor and pre-qualifies budget without anchoring your ceiling. Never
publish your maximum — only your minimum. The Signature tier at "Custom Quote" keeps
enterprise doors fully open.

→ Fix: Wix renders all content via JavaScript client-side. Google can process JS but it's deprioritized,

migration to WordPress, Webflow, or Next.js.
[CRITICAL] No Google Analytics GA4 or Google Search Console confirmed

→ Fix: Zero visibility into traffic sources, user behavior, or conversion paths. Install GA4 and connect
